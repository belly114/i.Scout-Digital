Facebook

How we can use the world's biggest social network to show Scouting.

Facebook is a great way to get your message out - but can also be used for internal communcations.
Use the resources available at the Print Centre or in Branding > Resources to jazz up your page with cover photos and profile pictures!
When using any form of Social Media account, always remember to use a secure password (typically more than 8 characters, using a mixture of uppercase, lowercase, symbols and numbers).

External Communications (Pages)

Using a Facebook Page to share your Fun, Challenge and Adventure within your Section works well, and can gain a lot of impressions (views). Here's how to get the most out of your page:

  • Keep it short and sweet. Example: "Time for a Cubs100 Party! [Photo]"
  • The page is there to tell the story of your Scouting experiences, not that Subs are due.
  • To reach the maximum audience, post during evenings or at weekends.
  • Spread the word! Tell parents, young people and the public about how they can Like your page.
  • A picture tells a thousand words. Be creative! Show your section having fun - see the Photography page. Always ensure you have photo consent.
  • Engage with your audience. Ask questions, respond to comments! Tip: Take a look at the Tone Of Voice page for some pointers.

    • Internal Communications (Groups)

    Another use of Facebook is Groups. Having a Secret Group is a great way of interacting with Parents or other leaders, where only invited members can view or post. When creating a group, remember to check your privacy settings, so that the group is secret, if you are intending to post sensitive information (meeting times, parent communication etc).
    Remember:

  • Even when posting in a closed group, do not use names, addresses or tag Young People.
